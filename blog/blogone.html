BLOG - SUMMER 2024

          I brought my camera to Governor's School knowing already that I wanted to capture this almost life-changing month. This frog was the first thing I captured on the Greensboro College campus, where this summer program was held. One evening, on a walk with new friends, I heard the ribbits of a frog coming from the fountain. Surely enough, we found the frog camouflaged among the rocks and crowded it to take pictures.

          Going on these evening walks quickly became one of my favorite parts of Governor's School. We'd talk about our experiences within and outside of the program and I got to know this new group of people I'm proud to call my friends. This day, while dropping off some friends at their dorm, we encountered a stunning sunset. With everyone stopping to take a picture I suddenly said, "Wait--just stay there." And through the sound of cascading laughs, I took this picture of one our first sunsets.


  "Don't shoot what it looks like, shoot what it feels like."
